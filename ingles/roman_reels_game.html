As the Emperor said:

In the age of endless war, numbers decide victory as much as steel. Chroniclers exaggerate, cowards diminish, and only wisdom reveals the truth.

Some claim I marched with MCX cohorts, boasting of impossible might. Others whisper that I brought merely XXV or LXX, as if fear guided my hand. Fools speak in extremes to mislead those who cannot count.

I tell you this: I gathered what was necessary — no more, no less. I relied upon the strength of XLV, supported by the precision of XLI and the certainty of XII. Together they formed a force neither bloated nor weak.

Read between the lies of war and the pride of the Emperor. Don't let yourself get distracted, and you shall uncover the true number that broke the enemy line.

I am the noble Xenon, emperor of Rome, ruler by decree of the Senate and by the sword of my legions.
